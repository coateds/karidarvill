Diamond Point Home

This two bedroom house is located on Diamond Point and overlooks a gorgeous panorama of the Strait of Juan de Fuca, Protection Island, Victoria, the Dungeness Lighthouse, Mt. Baker and other mountains.  There is a community beach usable by my guests.  You can walk for miles on the beach when the tide is out.  There are always eaglesflying around as well as seals, deer, and other wildlife to see - sometimes even Orcas!

The house has everything one could need to be comfortable.  There is a King bed in the master along with a pullout queen, and a queen in the small bedroom.  There is also a full bathroom on the second floor.  You can hear the waves and watch the sunrise on Baker from the master bedroom.  There are lots of books for your reading pleasure as well as a TV with DVDs and downstairs a Roku with a couple of subscription services for your viewing pleasure.  Additionally, wifi is available - the password is in the instruction booklet.  We also have children's toys over there though I would be cautious about very small children on the deck and bluff area.  Dogs are a case by case basis.  If I know your dog, it's fine.  I'm a bit leery about agreeing to dogs I don't know as the house has nice furniture etc.  I don't want things broken or chewed up etc.

Downstairs is a fully equipped kitchen, dining and living area, half bath, and good sized deck with a gas and charcoal grill.  There is a fireplace and plenty of wood as well as an outdoor firepit - please be cognizant of burn bans most summers.  There is a table and set of six chairs outside as well as other deck chairs.  The dining table has four main chairs and other chairs you can use to supplement.  We can easily get eight people around the table.

There is a lot of outdoor activities to do in the vicinity = hiking, snowshoeing, beach walking, biking etc.  Dancing is available most weekends to a live band at the casino.  Port Townsend is 35 minutes away and quite interesting to visit.  There is a bike trail, the Olympic Discovery Trail, at the top of the Diamond Point Road.  There are often festivals and other events.  Hurricane Ridge, with its breathtaking views and hiking/snowshoeing trails, is about a 75 minute drive one way.  Port Angeles is about 30 minutes.  The Dungeness Spit is about 20 minutes.  The town of Sequim is around 15 minutes away.
